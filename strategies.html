Trend Following

Trend following strategies aim to capitalize on market momentum by buying when prices are rising and selling when they are falling.

Definition: Trend following is a strategy that seeks to capture gains through the analysis of an asset's momentum.

Mean Reversion

Mean reversion strategies assume that prices will revert to their mean over time, allowing traders to profit from price corrections.

Key Points: This strategy is based on the idea that extreme price movements are often followed by a return to average levels.

Breakout Trading

Breakout trading involves entering a position when the price breaks through a key level of support or resistance, signaling a potential price movement.

Definition: A breakout occurs when the price moves outside a defined support or resistance level with increased volume.
